The Concept of Functional Medicine

Our staff takes an integrative health approach to meet the needs of the patients we serve, through a highly personalized model of healthcare delivery known as functional medicine. The concept of functional medicine, Dr. Hoyt addresses the interactions between the environment, genetics, and lifestyle and how these combined factors can influence chronic disease and long-term health. Your first appointment is likely to start with a highly detailed look at your medical history, followed by extensive lab work and testing of stool, saliva, blood, and urine. You might receive hair analysis and in-depth studies of food allergies and immune systems. We look for evidence of heavy metal substances, mold, Lyme, and chemical toxicity in your system. Autoimmune Disorders

Are you struggling with unexplained symptoms like fatigue, joint pain, and digestive issues? It could be a sign of an autoimmune disorder, a complex condition in which the body's immune system mistakenly attacks healthy cells and tissues.

Gastrotestinal

Do you experience frequent abdominal pain, bloating, or changes in bowel habits? These could be symptoms of a gastrointestinal disorder, a condition that affects the digestive system and can have a significant impact on your overall health and well-being.

Food allergy

Are you tired of constantly suffering from symptoms like hives, stomach pain, and difficulty breathing after eating certain foods? It could be a sign of a food allergy, a condition that affects millions of people and can cause severe reactions that range from uncomfortable to life-threatening.

Diabetese

Are you concerned about your blood sugar levels and experiencing symptoms like frequent urination, increased thirst, and fatigue? It could be a sign of diabetes, a chronic condition that affects millions of people worldwide and can have serious long-term health consequences if left untreated.

Lead Integrative/Preventive Specialist

Tracy Hoyt

Fuctional Medicine

Hoyt, a healthcare provider with over 20 years of experience, has a diverse skill set obtained through multiple disciplines. He completed his chiropractic training at LACC, now known as Southern California University of Health Sciences, and pursued a three-year postdoctoral residency in radiology, obtaining board certification. He also underwent a 6-month mentorship in MRI and CT.

Hoyt has reviewed thousands of cases and provided second opinions as a trained professional and consultant for other doctors. He has also served as an expert witness in legal matters. Furthermore, he has helped hundreds of occupationally injured patients as a Qualified Medical Examiner for the State of California Workman's Compensation, providing evaluations and written opinions related to disability and settlements.

Hoyt's passion for healing spine, joint, and sports injuries, as well as chronic pain, led him to develop an unprecedented blend of physiotherapy, rehab, nutrition, lab testing, diagnosis, and radiology. This integration expanded his reach and made him a sought after clinician. In the last three years, Hoyt obtained licensing in three states and worked as an administrator in nursing homes in the post-acute skilled nursing industry.

Through his experience, Hoyt has gained an enhanced appreciation for integrative and preventative care. Having recovered from a debilitating injury himself, he is currently practicing in a functional medicine model, utilizing integrative health and physical restoration to reverse chronic conditions in his patients.
